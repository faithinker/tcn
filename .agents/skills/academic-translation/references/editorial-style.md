# TCN Editorial Style

## House voice

Write for an international audience of researchers, educators, policymakers, cultural practitioners, and institutional partners. Aim for measured authority, intellectual clarity, and openness. Avoid promotional superlatives, inflated claims, corporate jargon, and literal Korean syntax.

Use American English spelling and punctuation in TCN-authored copy: `organization`, `center`, `program`, `adviser` only when an external official title requires it; otherwise use the glossary's `Advisor`. Preserve the exact spelling of official external names and quoted titles.

## Korean source editing

- Correct spacing, particles, agreement, duplicated ideas, ambiguous modifiers, and inconsistent defined terms.
- Prefer clear subjects and verbs over stacked nominalizations.
- Retain deliberate ceremonial rhythm in declarations and invitations.
- Do not simplify a voting threshold, legal actor, condition, exception, or scope.
- Present a meaning-changing Korean edit separately before treating it as the translation source.

## English translation

- Translate the intended proposition, logical relationship, and register rather than Korean word order.
- Prefer direct verbs and concrete subjects.
- Avoid repeated `in order to`, `through`, `based on`, `various`, `relevant`, and `promote` when a more precise construction is available.
- Preserve lists, parallel structure, emphasis, and paragraph boundaries when they carry rhetorical or legal meaning.
- Avoid introducing claims such as `leading`, `global standard`, `world-class`, or `unique` unless the Korean supports them.
- Use `digital and AI era`, not a mix of `digital/AI era`, `AI age`, and `digital age`, unless context requires a distinction.

## Semantic-equivalence checklist

Check every material sentence for:

- actor and recipient
- action and object
- purpose and causal relationship
- time, sequence, condition, and exception
- singular/plural and quantified scope
- permission, obligation, intention, and aspiration
- institutional body versus a particular meeting
- adopted fact versus future plan
- explicit information that disappeared or information added without support

For governance text, treat `shall`, `may`, `must`, `will`, and present-tense rules as materially different. Do not strengthen aspiration into obligation or weaken obligation into preference.

## Mechanics

- Use sentence case for headings unless an established UI pattern requires title case.
- Use the serial comma in English prose and lists.
- Use an en dash for ranges and an em dash for a deliberate break in prose.
- Keep abbreviations minimal; define `Transcultural Network (TCN)` on first use in a standalone long-form document.
- Preserve official diacritics and romanization.
- Do not translate URLs, slugs, IDs, dates, or machine-facing enum values.

## Review labels

Classify findings as:

- **Meaning:** omission, addition, changed agency, modality, scope, or factual content
- **Terminology:** conflict with the TCN glossary or an official name
- **Register:** unsuitable tone for the document and audience
- **English:** grammar, idiom, collocation, parallelism, or mechanics
- **Korean:** source ambiguity or correction needed before translation
- **Verification:** official rendering cannot be confirmed from available evidence
