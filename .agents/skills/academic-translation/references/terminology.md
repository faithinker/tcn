# TCN Terminology

This glossary is the single source of truth for recurring TCN terms. Use exact capitalization for defined bodies and titles. Use lowercase for generic references unless grammar requires otherwise.

## Countries, locations, and names

| Korean | Preferred English | Guidance |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 대한민국 | Republic of Korea | Use in addresses, bylaws, biographies, and formal copy. Use `Korea` only where brevity is intentional or it is part of an official name. Do not substitute `South Korea` in formal TCN copy. |
| 한국 | Korea | Use `Republic of Korea` when the context requires the formal country name or could be ambiguous. |
| 인천광역시 | Incheon | Use an official address romanization when giving the full postal address. |
| 강화군 | Ganghwa-gun | Preserve official romanization in addresses; `Ganghwa` is acceptable in running prose. |

Verify personal names, affiliations, universities, venues, and external job titles from official sources. Official names override house spelling.

## Academic versus scholarly

- Use **scholarly** for research, discourse, collaboration, communities, networks, exchange of ideas, publications, and events centered on knowledge production.
- Use **academic** for universities, degree programs, academic disciplines, academic credentials, academic years, and institutional exchange programs.
- Use **academia** for the sector or profession as a whole.
- Do not mechanically replace every `학술` with one word. Apply the semantic distinction above.

## TCN naming rule

Use `Transcultural Network` or `TCN` as the proper name. Use `the Network` as the preferred shortened institutional reference. Use `organization` only as a generic legal category. Do not call TCN `the Association` unless its official English name changes.
