# Document-Type Guidance

## Bylaws

Prioritize legal equivalence and internal consistency over elegance. Preserve the actor empowered to act, appointment or election mechanism, quorum or voting denominator, conditions, exceptions, defined bodies, and temporal effect. Use the glossary's governance terms consistently. Do not infer a legal rule missing from the Korean.

Read each article against related articles. In particular, cross-check membership classes, voting rights, composition of the Board, appointment authority, amendment thresholds, dissolution thresholds, and effective dates.

## Founding declaration

Preserve ceremonial dignity and argumentative progression without grandiose additions. Maintain the distinction between observations about the present, TCN's stated missions, commitments expressed with `will`, and hopes expressed with aspirational language. Natural repetition may support rhetoric; mechanical repetition should be edited.

## Invitations and event copy

Use welcoming, concise institutional prose. Distinguish the `Founding Assembly` as a governance meeting from a `Founding Ceremony` as a ceremonial event. Verify dates, venues, host institutions, speakers, titles, and country names against the structured data or an official source.

## Website and UI copy

Prioritize brevity, scanability, and consistent capitalization. Translate the function of labels rather than their literal form. Keep paired labels similar in information density. Preserve placeholders and accessible labels. Do not make UI copy more formal than the surrounding interface.

## Member profiles

Verify a person's romanized name, institution, department, and title from an official institutional profile whenever possible. Preserve official title spellings even when they differ from TCN house style. Avoid embellishing expertise or implying current affiliation when the source gives only a former role.

## News, history, and seminar records

Keep titles and summaries factual. Distinguish scheduled, completed, and postponed events. Preserve dates and participant counts. Use consistent ordinal naming for seminar series, and do not infer attendance or outcomes absent from the Korean record.
